I am a rising senior at Georgetown University in Washington, DC, majoring in Mathematics and German with a minor in Economics. I will graduate early after the Fall 2017 semester.

My greatest research interest is formal verification, the use of programming languages with dependent types such as Agda, Coq, and Idris to prove mathematical theorems. I am also interested in homotopy type theory and category theory, which form a theoretical basis for formal verification. Formal verification is an active area of mathematical research, and I would love to attend graduate school in mathematics to study it.

An interest of mine intimately related to formal verification is functional programming, where programs center around the evaluation of mathematical functions. The source code for this GitHub page is written in Haskell, probably the most popular functional programming languages. I also enjoy programming languages offering functional features in an otherwise imperative context such as Python and Rust.
